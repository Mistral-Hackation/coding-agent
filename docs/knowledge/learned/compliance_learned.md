## Thread Standards (2026-01-26 17:22)

*Source: Review Finding*

BSP G 1/2" thread specifications per EN ISO 228-1: Pitch = 1.814mm (14 TPI), Thread depth = 0.960mm. This is critical for EU compliance of pipe fittings. Common error: using arbitrary pitch values like 2.5mm which don't match any standard. NPT 1/2" has the same 14 TPI pitch but different thread angle (60° vs 55° for BSP).

## PED Compliance (2026-01-26 17:22)

*Source: Review Finding*

Threaded pipe fittings fall under PED 2014/68/EU as "pressure accessories". For small fittings (DN≤25), they typically fall under Article 4.3 (Sound Engineering Practice) or Category I, depending on pressure rating. Key requirements: (1) Pressure rating (PN) must be specified, (2) Material grade must reference EN standards (e.g., EN 12165 for brass), (3) Thread must comply with EN ISO 228-1 or EN ISO 7-1.

## Pipe Fittings Compliance (2026-01-26 17:53)

*Source: Review Finding*

Threaded pipe fittings under DN 25mm (such as 20x1/2" adapters) typically fall under PED Article 4.3 "Sound Engineering Practice" exemption when PS > 0.5 bar. CE marking may not be mandatory but documentation is required. Thread specifications should reference harmonized standards: EN ISO 228-1 (parallel, non-sealing), EN 10226-1/2 (taper, sealing), or ISO 7-1. Size designations mixing metric and imperial (e.g., "20x1/2") should be clarified for EU market.

## Furniture Regulations (2026-02-28 16:36)

*Source: WebSearch*

Office furniture (desks, tables) in the EU is NOT subject to CE marking requirements. Instead, they follow voluntary harmonized standards:

**Key EN Standards for Office Desks:**
- EN 527-1:2011 - Office furniture – Work tables and desks – Part 1: Dimensions (specifies standard dimensions including 720-730mm desk height)
- EN 527-2 - Safety, strength and durability requirements
- EN 527-3:2003 - Methods of test for stability and mechanical strength (withdrawn)

**Important Notes:**
1. CE marking is NOT required for standard office furniture (desks, tables, chairs)
2. REACH regulation applies if hazardous substances are present in materials
3. RoHS directive typically applies only to electrical/electronic equipment
4. General Product Safety Directive (2001/95/EC) applies as baseline safety requirement
5. Standard ergonomic desk height is 720-730mm per EN 527-1

**Review Approach for Furniture:**
- Skip detailed regulatory review (no CE, ATEX, PED required)
- Verify dimensions align with EN standards if applicable
- Furniture is a low-regulatory-burden product category

## Lifting Equipment EU Regulations (2026-03-01 13:03)

*Source: Review Finding*

LIFTING HOOKS AND ACCESSORIES - EU REGULATORY FRAMEWORK:

1. MACHINERY DIRECTIVE (2006/42/EC) - Primary Directive:
   - Lifting accessories (hooks, slings, shackles) fall under Annex IV - requiring EC type-examination
   - Article 2(d): "Lifting accessory" = component not attached to machine, allowing load to be gripped
   - Essential requirements in Annex I, Section 4 (Lifting Operations)
   - Section 4.1.2.3: Working Load Limit (WLL) must be marked
   - Section 4.3.2: Safety coefficient ≥ 4 for hooks (static loading), ≥ 5 for lifting chains

2. EN 1677-1: Forged steel components - Hooks with safety latches (Grade 8)
3. EN 1677-3: Forged steel components - Hooks without latches (Grade 8)
4. EN 13155: Cranes - Non-fixed load lifting attachments
5. EN ISO 12100: Safety of machinery - General principles

MANDATORY MARKING REQUIREMENTS:
- Working Load Limit (WLL) in tonnes or kg
- Material grade/designation
- CE marking
- Manufacturer identification
- Traceability marking

DESIGN CONSIDERATIONS:
- Safety factor typically 4:1 minimum for steel hooks
- Material: Grade 80 or Grade 100 alloy steel typical
- Fatigue considerations for cyclic loading
- NDT requirements for production hooks
